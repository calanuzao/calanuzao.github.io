Digital Signal Processing: In-Depth

What is Digital Signal Processing?

Digital Signal Processing (DSP) is a field of study that focuses on the analysis, manipulation, and synthesis of digital signals. It involves applying mathematical and computational algorithms to process discrete-time signals, which are sequences of numbers representing measurements or observations of physical phenomena. DSP has wide-ranging applications in areas such as audio and speech processing, image and video processing, telecommunications, biomedical engineering, and many other fields where data needs to be analyzed or transformed.

Key Concepts in DSP

  • Sampling and Quantization
  • Fourier Transform and Frequency Domain Analysis
  • Digital Filters (FIR and IIR)
  • Convolution and Correlation
  • Windowing and Spectral Analysis
  • Multirate Signal Processing
